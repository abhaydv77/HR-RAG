# Expense Reimbursement Policy

## Overview

TechCorp Inc. reimburses employees for legitimate business expenses incurred while performing their duties. This policy covers expense categories, limits, approval workflows, and submission procedures. All expenses must be actual, reasonable, and properly documented.

## General Principles

- All expenses must be pre-approved by the reporting manager or department head unless explicitly stated otherwise in this policy.
- Expenses must be business-related and necessary for the employee to perform their role.
- Personal expenses must never be claimed as business expenses.
- Deliberately claiming false expenses is a serious disciplinary offense and may lead to termination.
- All amounts in this policy are in USD unless stated otherwise.

## Expense Categories and Limits

### Travel Expenses

#### Air Travel
- **Domestic flights (under 4 hours)**: Economy class only. No pre-approval needed for tickets under $400.
- **Domestic flights (over 4 hours)**: Premium economy permitted. Pre-approval from department head required for tickets over $600.
- **International flights (any duration)**: Economy class only unless the flight exceeds 8 hours, in which case Premium Economy or Business class is permitted. All international travel requires pre-approval from the department head AND the VP of the division.
- **Booking**: All flights must be booked through the company's designated travel portal. Booking outside the portal requires justification and may result in reduced reimbursement.

#### Ground Transportation
- **Personal vehicle**: Reimbursed at $0.58 per mile. Parking fees and tolls are additionally reimbursed with receipts.
- **Ride sharing (Uber/Lyft/taxi)**: Reimbursed for business purposes only. No limit per ride, but expenses over $50 require an explanation.
- **Rental cars**: Economy or midsize only. Requires pre-approval for rentals exceeding $80 per day. Gas is reimbursed with receipts.

#### Lodging
- **Standard hotels**: Up to $200 per night (domestic) or $300 per night (international).
- **Extended stays**: For stays of 5+ nights, approval from department head is required. Accommodations other than hotels (Airbnb, serviced apartments) may be used if more cost-effective.
- **Incidentals**: Room service, mini-bar, and personal entertainment are NOT reimbursable. Laundry service is reimbursable for stays exceeding 5 nights.

### Meals and Entertainment

#### Meals While Traveling
- **Breakfast**: Up to $15
- **Lunch**: Up to $20
- **Dinner**: Up to $40
- **Total daily limit**: $75 per day
- **Receipts required**: For any single expense over $25
- **Alcohol**: Reimbursable only when entertaining clients or when it is standard practice in the business context. Maximum $15 per person for alcoholic beverages.

#### Client Entertainment
- **Meals with clients**: Up to $100 per person. Requires pre-approval from department head if the total exceeds $500.
- **Event tickets**: Sporting events, concerts, or shows — up to $150 per ticket. Requires pre-approval from department head.
- **Gifts for clients**: Up to $100 per client per year. Must be appropriate and not cash or cash equivalents.

### Office Supplies and Equipment

#### Supplies
- **Routine supplies** (stationery, printer paper, postage): Up to $50 per month without pre-approval.
- **Software subscriptions**: Up to $30 per month per tool without pre-approval. Tools must be approved by IT for security compliance.

#### Equipment
- **Items under $100**: May be purchased without pre-approval.
- **Items $100–$500**: Require manager approval.
- **Items over $500**: Require manager AND department head approval, and must be purchased through IT procurement.
- **Company policy**: Employees should first check if the equipment can be provided by the company before purchasing.

### Professional Development

#### Conferences
- **Registration fees**: Up to $1,000 per conference without VP approval.
- **Travel + accommodation + registration combined**: Up to $2,500 per conference. Requires VP approval beyond that.
- **Limit**: 2 conferences per employee per financial year.

#### Training and Certifications
- **Online courses**: Up to $500 per course. Pre-approval from manager required.
- **Certification exams**: Fully reimbursed upon passing. Exam retakes are reimbursed at 50%.
- **Books**: Up to $100 per month without pre-approval.

## Expense Submission and Approval Process

### Submission Timeline
- Expenses must be submitted within **30 days** of being incurred.
- Late submissions (30–60 days): Requires manager's acknowledgment.
- Submissions beyond 60 days: Requires department head approval and may be denied.

### Documentation Requirements
Every expense report must include:
1. Itemized receipt showing date, vendor, amount, and items purchased
2. Business purpose of the expense
3. Names of attendees (for meals/entertainment)
4. Project or client code (if applicable)

Digital copies of receipts are accepted. Original physical receipts must be retained for audit purposes for 12 months.

### Approval Workflow
1. **Under $200**: Auto-approved after manager review
2. **$200–$1,000**: Requires manager approval
3. **$1,000–$5,000**: Requires manager AND department head approval
4. **Over $5,000**: Requires manager, department head, AND VP approval

### Reimbursement Timeline
- Approved expenses are processed within **5–7 business days** of submission.
- Expenses submitted with errors or missing documentation will be returned for correction and will not be processed until complete.

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are never reimbursable:
- Commuting to and from the regular workplace
- Personal grooming, haircuts, or cosmetics
- Parking tickets, traffic fines, or legal penalties
- Health and wellness expenses (gym memberships, massages)
- Childcare or eldercare (even when traveling)
- Personal travel insurance
- Upgrades for personal comfort (seat upgrades, room upgrades)
- Alcoholic beverages without client presence

## Expense Audit

TechCorp conducts quarterly audits on a random sample of 10% of expense reports. Employees found to have submitted ineligible expenses will be:
- **First offense**: Required to repay the amount + written warning
- **Second offense**: Repayment + final written warning + loss of expense privileges for 3 months
- **Third offense**: Repayment + disciplinary action up to termination
