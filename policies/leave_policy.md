# Leave Policy

## Overview

TechCorp Inc. provides three types of leave to all full-time employees: Sick Leave (SL), Casual Leave (CL), and Earned Leave (EL). This policy document outlines the rules governing accrual, usage, carry-forward, and encashment of all leave types.

## Sick Leave

### Accrual
Employees accrue 1.5 days of sick leave per completed month of service. Sick leave credits are credited to the employee's leave account on the first day of each month. New joiners receive a pro-rated credit in their first month based on the number of days remaining in that month.

### Maximum Balance
The maximum sick leave balance that can be accumulated is 30 days. Once an employee reaches 30 days, no further sick leave accrual occurs until the balance drops below the maximum.

### Usage
Sick leave may be taken for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments (doctor visits, dental checkups)
- Quarantine due to contagious illness
- Care of an immediate family member who is ill (up to 3 days per occurrence)
- Mental health days (up to 2 days per quarter without a medical certificate)

### Documentation Requirements
- **1–2 consecutive days**: Self-declaration is sufficient. No medical certificate required.
- **3 consecutive days or more**: A valid medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner must be submitted within 3 working days of returning to work.
- **More than 5 consecutive days**: The employee must also inform HR and may be referred to the company's occupational health provider.

### Carry Forward
Unused sick leave may be carried forward to the next calendar year, subject to the maximum balance cap of 30 days. Sick leave is not encashable upon resignation or separation.

## Casual Leave

### Accrual
Employees accrue 1.25 days of casual leave per completed month of service. Casual leave is credited on the first day of each month.

### Maximum Balance
The maximum casual leave balance is 15 days. Unlike sick leave, casual leave does NOT accrue further once the cap is reached.

### Usage
Casual leave is intended for:
- Personal errands and appointments
- Family events and celebrations
- Short breaks and travel
- Any personal reason that does not qualify as sick leave

### Notice and Approval
- **1 day**: Notice to the reporting manager at least 24 hours in advance is required.
- **2–3 days**: Written approval from the reporting manager is required at least 2 working days in advance.
- **More than 3 days**: Written approval from both the reporting manager and the department head is required at least 5 working days in advance.

### Carry Forward
Up to 5 days of unused casual leave may be carried forward to the next calendar year. Any casual leave balance beyond 5 days at the end of the calendar year is forfeited.

## Earned Leave

### Accrual
Employees accrue 1.75 days of earned leave per completed month of service. This is the primary leave type for planned time off and vacations.

### Eligibility for Availment
- **Probation period**: No earned leave may be availed during the probation period (first 6 months). Leave accrued during probation becomes available from the 7th month onward.
- **Minimum balance**: Employees must maintain a minimum balance of 3 earned leave days at all times, except in emergencies with HR approval.

### Usage
Earned leave can be taken as a continuous block. Requests for blocks longer than 10 working days require approval from the department head and HR.

### Notice Period for Earned Leave
- **1–3 days**: 3 working days' notice
- **4–10 days**: 2 weeks' notice
- **More than 10 days**: 1 month's notice

### Carry Forward
Unused earned leave may be carried forward indefinitely, subject to a maximum balance of 45 days. Any balance above 45 days at the end of the calendar year is automatically forfeited.

### Encashment
Upon resignation or separation, earned leave is encashed at the employee's current basic salary rate, subject to a maximum of 30 days. Encashment is paid out in the final settlement.

## Leave Application Process

All leave applications must be submitted through TechCorp's HR portal. The workflow is as follows:

1. Employee submits leave request in the portal with dates, type, and reason.
2. The system checks leave balance automatically and flags if insufficient.
3. The request is routed to the reporting manager for approval (or rejection).
4. For leaves longer than 3 days, the department head is added as an additional approver.
5. Once approved, the leave is deducted from the employee's available balance.
6. For urgent/emergency leave, employees should call their manager directly and submit the portal request within 24 hours.

## Holidays and Weekends

National and company-declared holidays are not counted as leave. Weekends (Saturday and Sunday) that fall within a leave period are not counted as leave days. For example, a leave from Friday to Monday counts as 2 leave days (Friday and Monday), not 4.

## Unauthorized Absence

Any absence without approved leave is treated as unauthorized absence. Consequences:
- **1–2 days**: Written warning from the reporting manager
- **3–5 days**: Final written warning and deduction of pay for those days
- **More than 5 consecutive days**: Treated as a disciplinary matter and may lead to termination of employment. The employee is given an opportunity to explain the absence before any decision is made.
