# Code of Conduct

## Overview

TechCorp Inc. is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, and inclusive workplace. This Code of Conduct outlines the standards of behavior expected from all employees, contractors, and anyone representing TechCorp.

## Core Values

1. **Integrity**: We act honestly and ethically in all business dealings.
2. **Respect**: We treat everyone with dignity and respect, regardless of their background, identity, or position.
3. **Accountability**: We take responsibility for our actions and their impact on others.
4. **Collaboration**: We work together across teams and departments to achieve shared goals.
5. **Innovation**: We encourage creativity and calculated risk-taking.

## Professional Conduct

### Workplace Behavior
All employees are expected to:
- Communicate respectfully in all interactions, whether in-person, via email, chat, or video calls
- Avoid gossip, rumor-spreading, or malicious conversations about colleagues
- Maintain professional boundaries with colleagues and avoid romantic or intimate relationships with direct reports
- Refrain from using offensive language, making derogatory comments, or engaging in personal attacks
- Respect others' time by starting and ending meetings on schedule
- Keep shared workspaces clean and tidy

### Harassment and Discrimination
TechCorp has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment and discrimination of any kind. This includes but is not limited to:
- Harassment based on race, color, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic
- Sexual harassment, including unwelcome advances, comments, or physical contact
- Bullying, intimidation, or threatening behavior
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith

Any employee who experiences or witnesses harassment or discrimination should report it immediately to HR or through the confidential ethics hotline.

### Conflicts of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict (or appear to conflict) with the interests of TechCorp. Examples include:
- Engaging in outside employment or consulting that competes with TechCorp
- Having a financial interest in a supplier, vendor, or competitor
- Hiring or supervising a family member or close personal friend
- Using company time, equipment, or resources for personal business ventures

Any actual or potential conflict of interest must be disclosed to your manager and HR.

### Confidentiality and Data Protection
Employees must protect TechCorp's confidential information and that of its clients and partners. This includes:
- Not sharing confidential information with unauthorized persons inside or outside the company
- Not discussing confidential matters in public places (elevators, cafeterias, public transit)
- Securing physical and digital documents containing confidential information
- Reporting any data breach or security incident immediately to IT and HR
- Complying with all applicable data protection laws, including GDPR and CCPA where applicable

Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends.

### Use of Company Resources
Company resources (laptops, software, internet, office supplies) must be used primarily for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted as long as it:
- Does not interfere with work responsibilities
- Does not consume excessive bandwidth or resources
- Does not access inappropriate or illegal content
- Does not violate any other company policy

## Social Media and External Communications

Employees are free to maintain personal social media accounts but must:
- Clearly state that views are personal and not those of TechCorp when discussing industry topics
- Not disclose confidential company information
- Not represent themselves as speaking on behalf of TechCorp without authorization
- Not engage in online harassment, trolling, or defamatory behavior that could reflect poorly on TechCorp
- Refrain from posting about internal company matters, meetings, or decisions

## Compliance with Laws

All employees must comply with applicable laws and regulations in the jurisdictions where TechCorp operates, including but not limited to:
- Labor and employment laws
- Anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws
- Intellectual property laws
- Securities laws (for employees with access to material non-public information)
- Trade sanctions and export control laws

## Reporting Violations

Employees who witness or suspect a violation of this Code of Conduct should report it through one of the following channels:
1. **Direct manager**: For minor concerns or questions
2. **HR department**: For any concern, especially harassment, discrimination, or retaliation
3. **Ethics hotline**: A confidential, anonymous reporting channel managed by an external provider. Phone: 1-800-555-0199 or online at ethics.techcorp.com

TechCorp prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation.

## Disciplinary Actions

Violations of this Code of Conduct will result in disciplinary action proportionate to the severity of the violation, which may include:
- Verbal counseling and coaching
- Written warning
- Final written warning
- Suspension with or without pay
- Termination of employment
- Legal action, if laws were violated

Previous conduct and the employee's history will be taken into account when determining appropriate action.
