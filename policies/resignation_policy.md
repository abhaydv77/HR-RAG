# Resignation and Notice Period Policy

## Overview

This policy outlines the procedures and expectations when an employee resigns from TechCorp Inc., including notice periods, handover requirements, final settlement, and exit formalities.

## Resignation Notice Period

### Standard Notice Period
All full-time employees are required to serve a notice period of **60 calendar days** (approximately 2 months) from the date the resignation letter is submitted and acknowledged by HR.

### Probation Period Notice
Employees who are still in their probation period (first 6 months) are required to serve a notice period of **15 calendar days**.

### Contract/Temporary Employees
Contract and temporary employees must provide **30 calendar days' notice**, unless the employment contract specifies a different period.

## Notice Period Buyout

Employees may request a buyout (waiver) of their notice period, which means leaving before the notice period ends. This is at the company's discretion and is subject to the following conditions:

- **Buyout by employee**: The employee may pay an amount equivalent to their basic salary for the unserved notice period. This is deducted from their final settlement.
- **Buyout by company (garden leave)**: TechCorp may, at its discretion, ask the employee to leave immediately and pay them salary in lieu of the notice period (garden leave). In this case, the employee is paid for the full notice period but is not required to work.

## Resignation Process

### Step 1: Submission
The employee must submit a formal resignation letter to their reporting manager, with a copy to HR. The letter should include:
- Intended last working day (considering the notice period)
- Reason for resignation (optional but appreciated for exit analysis)

### Step 2: Acceptance and Acknowledgment
- HR acknowledges receipt of the resignation within 2 working days.
- The manager and HR schedule a discussion with the employee within 5 working days.
- A formal acceptance letter is issued, confirming the last working day.

### Step 3: Counter-Offer
TechCorp reserves the right to make a counter-offer to retain the employee. If the employee accepts the counter-offer, the resignation is withdrawn. Counter-offers are considered on a case-by-case basis and are not guaranteed.

### Step 4: Knowledge Transfer and Handover
During the notice period, the employee is expected to:
- Complete a knowledge transfer document for their role and ongoing projects
- Train a back-up or replacement colleague on their key responsibilities
- Hand over all code, documents, passwords, and access credentials
- Participate in exit interviews if requested
- Ensure all pending work items are either completed or transitioned

### Step 5: Exit Formalities
On or before the last working day, the employee must:
- Return all company property (laptop, badge, access card, keys, company credit card)
- Settle any outstanding advances or dues
- Complete the exit clearance form signed by IT, Finance, Admin, and HR
- Remove personal belongings from desk and lockers
- Cancel standing accesses (door access, parking, software accounts)

## Final Settlement

The final settlement is processed within **30 calendar days** of the last working day and includes:
- Salary for days worked in the final month
- Encashment of earned leave (up to 30 days, at current basic salary rate)
- Any variable pay or bonus earned (pro-rated as per the bonus plan)
- Deductions for: notice period buyout (if applicable), outstanding loans/advances, unreturned company property, taxes as applicable

A detailed breakup of the final settlement is provided to the employee via email.

## Resignation Withdrawal

An employee may withdraw their resignation within **7 calendar days** of submitting it, provided:
- The withdrawal is submitted in writing to HR
- The manager and HR agree to the withdrawal
- No counter-offer has been extended and declined

Withdrawals after 7 days or after the notice period buyout has been processed are at the sole discretion of the company.

## Exit Interview

All departing employees are requested to participate in an exit interview (in-person or virtual). The exit interview is an opportunity for employees to provide honest feedback about their experience at TechCorp. All feedback is confidential and used for organizational improvement.

## Rehire Policy

Former employees who resigned in good standing may be considered for rehire after a cooling-off period of **6 months** from their last working day. Employees who were terminated for cause or who resigned while under disciplinary investigation are not eligible for rehire.

## Early Departure Restrictions

Certain employees may be restricted from early departure (i.e., the company may not accept a shorter notice period) if they:
- Hold a critical client-facing role with active engagements
- Are the sole subject matter expert on a critical system or project
- Are under active investigation for misconduct
- Have access to highly sensitive or classified information
- Hold a position for which a replacement cannot be found within the notice period

In such cases, HR will communicate the restriction in writing within 7 days of receiving the resignation.

## Garden Leave

TechCorp may place a resigning employee on garden leave during all or part of the notice period. During garden leave:
- The employee continues to receive their full salary and benefits
- The employee is not required to attend the office or perform work
- The employee must remain available for handover calls or questions if needed
- The employee's access to company systems may be revoked
- Garden leave does not reduce the notice period
