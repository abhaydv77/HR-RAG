# Work From Home / Hybrid Work Policy

## Overview

TechCorp Inc. follows a hybrid work model that balances in-office collaboration with remote flexibility. This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees unless their role requires 100% on-site presence (as determined by their department head).

## Work Models

### Fully Remote Roles
Some roles at TechCorp are designated as fully remote. These are typically individual contributor roles in Engineering, Design, and Customer Support where in-person presence is not essential. Fully remote employees may work from anywhere within the country but must maintain the same working hours as their team's core time zone.

### Hybrid Roles
Most roles at TechCorp follow a hybrid model with the following expectations:
- **Minimum in-office days**: 3 days per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday are considered "core days" when all hybrid employees are expected in the office)
- **Flexible days**: Monday and Friday are flexible — employees may choose to work from home or come into the office.
- **Team discretion**: Individual teams may adjust these days with approval from the department head, as long as the minimum 3-day average is maintained over a month.

### On-Site Roles
Roles in Manufacturing, Lab Operations, Facilities, and Security are designated as on-site and require daily attendance at the office location.

## Requesting Work From Home

### Planned WFH
For flexible days (Monday/Friday), no formal request is needed — employees simply indicate their location in the team calendar by 9:00 AM on the day.

### Ad-Hoc WFH on Core Days
Employees may occasionally work from home on a core day (Tuesday–Thursday) for specific reasons such as:
- Home service appointments (plumber, electrician, etc.)
- Expected package deliveries that require signature
- Minor personal illness (not contagious — use sick leave if contagious)
- Caregiver responsibilities (elder care, child care during school closures)

For ad-hoc WFH on core days, employees must:
1. Inform their reporting manager by 8:00 AM on the day
2. Mark their status as "WFH" in the team calendar
3. Ensure they are reachable during working hours

Ad-hoc WFH on core days is limited to 4 days per month.

### Extended WFH (More Than 5 Consecutive Working Days)
Extended work-from-home arrangements require formal approval from the department head and HR. Requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance and should include:
- Reason for extended WFH
- Expected duration
- Plan for staying connected with the team
- Any equipment or connectivity requirements

Extended WFH may be approved for situations such as:
- Temporary relocation or family commitments
- Medical recovery (post-surgery, etc.)
- Special project circumstances

## Equipment and Connectivity

### Company-Provided Equipment
All employees, regardless of work model, are provided with a company laptop. Employees approved for WFH on a regular basis may also request:
- An external monitor and docking station
- A headset with noise cancellation
- An ergonomic chair (for fully remote employees)

### Internet Connectivity
Employees working from home are responsible for ensuring a stable internet connection. If connectivity issues prevent work for more than 2 hours on a given day, the employee should:
1. Attempt to resolve the issue (hotspot, alternate location, etc.)
2. Inform their manager and team about the disruption
3. If working from a co-working space, the company provides a monthly stipend of $150

TechCorp provides a monthly internet reimbursement of $50 for all hybrid and remote employees.

## Working Hours and Availability

### Core Hours
TechCorp's core working hours are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM local time. All employees must be available during these hours regardless of their work location.

### Flexible Start and End
Employees may start as early as 7:00 AM and end as late as 7:00 PM, as long as they complete 8.5 working hours per day (8 hours of work + 0.5 hour lunch break).

### Availability Expectations
While working from home, employees must:
- Be responsive on Slack/Teams within 15 minutes during core hours
- Have their status indicator set to "Available" during working hours
- Be available for video calls as scheduled
- Not schedule personal appointments during core hours

## Data Security and Confidentiality

When working from home, employees must adhere to the following security requirements:
- Company laptops must be connected to the corporate VPN when accessing any internal system
- Work must not be performed from public Wi-Fi networks (coffee shops, airports, etc.) without VPN
- Screens must not be visible to unauthorized persons
- Company documents must not be printed at home unless specifically authorized
- Any security incident (lost/stolen laptop, suspected breach) must be reported to IT within 1 hour

## Weekly Team Coordination

To maintain team cohesion in a hybrid setup, all teams are expected to:
- Conduct a weekly "sync-up" meeting on either Tuesday or Wednesday when most members are in-office
- Use video-on for all team meetings regardless of location
- Maintain a shared team calendar showing each member's location for the day
- Rotate meeting times to accommodate different time zones if team members are distributed

## Policy Violations

Violations of the WFH policy (such as being consistently unavailable during core hours, misrepresenting work location, or security breaches) will be addressed as follows:
- **First violation**: Verbal reminder from the reporting manager
- **Second violation**: Written warning
- **Third violation**: The employee's WFH privileges may be revoked for a period of 1–3 months
- **Serious violations** (security breaches, fraud): Disciplinary action up to and including termination
